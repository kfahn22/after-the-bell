Let us imagine that you are trying to decide what to have for lunch. You have the following items available:

  • mustard
  • mayo
  • turkey
  • roast beef
  • swiss cheese
  • egg salad
  • grape jelly
  • yogurt
  • peanut butter
  • wheat bread
  • white bread
  • pita bread
  • rice

    • Let us also imagine that you are feeling a bit adventurous (or indecisive) and decide to randomly choose one item. Your first choice ends up being peanut butter. The question is would you be happy with all the remaining options or would you decide to limit yourself to a much smaller subject of food items, such as bread and jelly. We would say that your first choose has COLLAPSED your remaining options. The computer algorithm we are going to use to generate digital quilts operates in much the same way. We provide the program with a number of tile options and the computer randomly chooses a tile and places it in the quilt. The set of instructions we provide to the computer then dictate what tiles can go next to the first tile.
